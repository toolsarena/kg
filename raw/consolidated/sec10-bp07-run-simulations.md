# SEC10-BP07 Run simulations

As organizations grow and evolve over time, so does the threat landscape, making it important to
continually review your incident response capabilities. Running simulations (also known as game
days) is one method that can be used to perform this assessment. Simulations use real-world
security event scenarios designed to mimic a threat actor’s tactics, techniques, and procedures
(TTPs) and allow an organization to exercise and evaluate their incident response capabilities by
responding to these mock cyber events as they might occur in reality.
Benefits of establishing this best practice: Simulations have a variety of benefits:
• Validating cyber readiness and developing the confidence of your incident responders.
• Testing the accuracy and efficiency of tools and workflows.
• Refining communication and escalation methods aligned with your incident response plan.
• Providing an opportunity to respond to less common vectors.
Level of risk exposed if this best practice is not established: Medium
