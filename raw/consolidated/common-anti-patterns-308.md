# Common anti-patterns:

• Granting excessive or broad access privileges to identities upfront, beyond what is initially
required.
• Not reviewing and adjusting access privileges as identities' roles and responsibilities change over
time.
• Leaving inactive or terminated identities with active access privileges. This increases the risk of
unauthorized access.
• Not leveraging automation to manage the lifecycle of identities.
Level of risk exposed if this best practice is not established: Medium


# Common anti-patterns:

• Not keeping an inventory of shared resources.
