# Implementation steps

1. Document the existing processes and procedures.
a. Review and keep them up-to-date.
b. Identify an owner for each process or procedure.
c. Place them under version control.
d. Where possible, share processes and procedures across workloads and environments that
share architectural designs.
2. Establish mechanisms for feedback and improvement.
a. Define policies for how frequently processes should be reviewed.
b. Define processes for reviewers and approvers.
c. Implement issues or a ticketing queue for feedback to be provided and tracked.
d. Whereever possible, processes and procedures should have pre-approval and risk classification
from a change approval board (CAB).
3. Verify that processes and procedures are accessible and discoverable by those who need to run
them.
a. Use tags to indicate where the process and procedures can be accessed for the workload.
b. Use meaningful error and event messaging to indicate the appropriate processes or
procedures to address an issue.
c. Use wikis and document management, and make processes and procedures searchable
consistently accross the organization.
