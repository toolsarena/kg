# Common anti-patterns:

• You only monitor system-level metrics to gain insight into your workload and don’t understand
business impacts to those metrics.
• You assume that your KPIs are already being published and shared as standard metric data.
• You do not define a quantitative, measurable KPI.
• You do not align KPIs with business goals or strategies.
Benefits of establishing this best practice: Identifying specific KPIs that represent workload health
and performance helps align teams on their priorities and define successful business outcomes.
Sharing those metrics with all departments provides visibility and alignment on thresholds,
expectations, and business impact.
Level of risk exposed if this best practice is not established: High
