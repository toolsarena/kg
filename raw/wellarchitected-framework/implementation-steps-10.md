# Implementation steps

1. Define policies, standards, and expectations for your organization.
a. Ensure wide adoption and understanding of policies, expectations, and standards.
2. Encourage, train, and empower workers for early and frequent escalation when standards are
not met.
3. Organizationally acknowledge that early and frequent escalation is the best practice. Accept that
escalations may prove to be unfounded, and that it is better to have the opportunity to prevent
an incident then to miss that opportunity by not escalating.
a. Build a mechanism for escalation (like an Andon cord system).
b. Have documented procedures defining when and how escalation should occur.
c. Define the series of people with increasing authority to take or approve action, as well as each
stakeholder's contact information.
4. When escalation occurs, it should continue until the team member is satisfied that the risk has
been mitigated through actions driven from leadership.
a. Escalations should include:
i. Description of the situation, and the nature of the risk
