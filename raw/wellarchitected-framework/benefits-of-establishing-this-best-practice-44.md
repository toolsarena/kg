# Benefits of establishing this best practice:

• Limits the scope of impact for unauthorized access to one part of the workload.
• Provides a higher level of assurance that actions are only performed by authenticated entities.
• Improves decoupling of services by clearly defining and enforcing intended data transfer
interfaces.
• Enhances monitoring, logging, and incident response through request attribution and well-
defined communication interfaces.
• Provides defense-in-depth for your workloads by combining network controls with
authentication and authorization controls.
Level of risk exposed if this best practice is not established: Low
